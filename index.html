EN    The original idea with the house was to center it around open and empty floors, where flexiblitiy and transformation was key to the operation. The escalators was installed as an afterthought.

EN    Kulturhuset is open from 7 in the morning until 9 in the evening every day of the week. The original idea was that workers should have the opportunity to visit the house regardless of ones working hours.

SV    Detta är den digitala delen av guiden Inside looking out / Outside looking in. En alternativ guide till Kulturhuset. Peter Celsing som ritade kulturhuset menade att huset skulle vara som en skärm där man kunde se verksamheten inne i byggnaden, en kontaktyta mot omvärlden. I och med åren så har kulturhuset förändrats, man har installerat rulltrappor, tagit bort den stora läsesalongen med stolarna riktade mot fönstren, skärmat av och koncentrerat aktiviteterna i huset kring teater och restaurang. Likt ett köpcenter med olika inhyrda butiker så har verksamheten växt fram.

Denna digitala del går endast att se utanför kulturhuset för att uppmuntra till reflektion kring kulturhuset som del av ett större sammanhang.

Här finner du bilder tagna i kulturhuset med linsen riktat in mot huset. Guiden bygger på personliga reflektioner och iakttagelser gjorda under 6 veckor på och kring kulturhuset.

EN    This is the digital section of the Inside Look Out / Outside Looking In wizard. An alternative guide to the Cultural House. Peter Celsing, who designed the cultural house, meant that the house would be like a screen where you could see the business inside the building, a contact area to the outside world. Over the years, the cultural center has changed, you have installed escalators, removed the large reading lounge with the chairs facing the windows, screened off and concentrated activities in the house around the theater and restaurant. Like a shopping mall with various rented stores, the business has grown.

This digital part can only be seen outside the cultural house to encourage reflection around the cultural center as part of a broader context.

Here you will find pictures taken in the cultural house with the lens directed towards the house. The guide is based on personal reflections and observations made for 6 weeks at and around the cultural house.
